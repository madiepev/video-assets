Understanding Wave Interference

🌟 Constructive Interference

When waves meet and their peaks align, they add together to create brighter, more intense areas. The waves reinforce each other, creating maximum amplitude.

🌑 Destructive Interference

When a wave's peak meets another wave's trough, they cancel each other out, creating darker, quieter areas. The waves subtract from each other, reducing amplitude.

Real-world examples: This same principle explains noise-canceling headphones, radio dead zones, and the colorful patterns in soap bubbles!
